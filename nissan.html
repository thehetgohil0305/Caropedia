Nissan

  • Nissan is a Japanese automotive giant known for its engineering excellence, innovation, and performance-driven vehicles. Founded in 1933, Nissan has built a strong reputation in motorsports and road cars, offering a diverse range of vehicles from high-performance sports cars to reliable sedans.
    Yoshisuke Aikawa (Founder)
  • As of 2025, the CEO of Nissan is Makoto Uchida, driving the company towards technological advancements, sustainability, and electrification.
    Makoto Uchida (CEO)
  • Nissan has a deep-rooted history in motorsports, particularly in Super GT and endurance racing, where the Nissan NISMO Team has established itself as a dominant force. With a strong legacy of engineering excellence, the team is committed to developing high-performance, aerodynamically advanced race cars that compete at the highest levels of motorsport. Focused on innovation, precision, and strategic excellence, Nissan aims to achieve consistent victories and reinforce its reputation as a powerhouse in the racing world.
  • Nissan has a rich history in motorsport, with legendary drivers contributing to its racing heritage. The brand has achieved significant success in endurance racing, including multiple victories at the prestigious Le Mans 24 Hours and dominance in Super GT. Nissan’s race cars, such as the iconic R32 GT-R "Godzilla" and the Nissan GT-R NISMO GT500, have cemented its reputation for performance and durability on some of the world's most challenging circuits.
  • Nissan's current road cars are renowned for their blend of performance, innovation, and reliability. Iconic models like the GT-R, Z, and Ariya showcase the brand’s commitment to engineering excellence, aerodynamics, and advanced technology. With a focus on power, efficiency, and driving excitement, Nissan continues to set benchmarks in the world of sports cars, performance vehicles, and electric mobility.
