Ferrari

  • Ferrari is an Italian luxury sports car manufacturer based in Maranello, Italy. It was founded in 1939 by Enzo Ferrari as Auto Avio Costruzioni and officially became "Ferrari" in 1947. The brand is known for its iconic red cars, innovative engineering, and unparalleled success in racing.
    Enzo Ferrari (Founder)
  • The current CEO of Ferrari is Benedetto Vigna, a leader focused on pushing the brand towards innovation while maintaining its racing heritage.
    Benedetto Vigna (CEO)
  • Ferrari has a deep-rooted history in motorsports, particularly in Formula 1, where the Scuderia Ferrari team stands as the most iconic and successful in the sport’s history. With a strong legacy of engineering excellence, the team is committed to developing high-performance, aerodynamically advanced race cars that compete at the pinnacle of motorsport. Focused on innovation, precision, and strategic excellence, Ferrari aims to achieve consistent race victories and championship titles, reinforcing its status as a dominant force in Formula 1.
  • Ferrari has a rich history in motorsport, with legendary drivers contributing to its racing heritage. The brand has achieved significant success in endurance racing, including multiple overall victories at the prestigious Le Mans 24 Hours. Ferrari’s race cars, such as the 250 GTO and the 499P Hypercar, have cemented its reputation for performance, innovation, and durability on some of the world's most challenging circuits.
  • Ferrari's current road cars are celebrated for their blend of performance, innovation, and Italian craftsmanship. Iconic models like the SF90 Stradale, 296 GTB, and Purosangue showcase the brand’s commitment to aerodynamics, cutting-edge engineering, and driving excellence. With a focus on power, precision, and an exhilarating driving experience, Ferrari continues to set benchmarks in the world of supercars, hypercars, and performance-driven grand tourers.
  • Here are some of Ferrari's greatest creations :
    • The Ferrari LaFerrari is a limited-production hybrid sports car that was unveiled at the 2013 Geneva Motor Show. It is powered by a 6.3-liter V12 engine.
    • Production: 2013-2016
      Engine: 6.3L V12 with KERS (HY-KERS) electric motor
      Power: 949 hp (708 kW; 962 PS)
      0-100 kph: 2.4 seconds
      Top Speed: 354 km/h
      Type: Hybrid Hypercar
      Notable Feature: Ferrari’s first hybrid hypercar combining performance with efficiency
    • The Ferrari F40 is a mid-engined, two-seater supercar produced by Ferrari from 1987 to 1992. It was the last Ferrari to be powered by a naturally aspirated engine.
    • Production: 1987-1992
      Engine: 2.9L Twin-Turbo V8
      Power: 478 hp (356 kW)
      0-100 kph: 4.1 seconds
      Top Speed: 323 km/h
      Type: Supercar
      Notable Feature: Built to celebrate Ferrari’s 40th anniversary and the last car personally approved by Enzo Ferrari
    • The Ferrari 250 GTO is a classic car that was produced by Ferrari from 1962 to 1964. It is considered one of the most beautiful cars ever made and is highly sought after by collectors. The designer of this car is Giotto Bizzarrini.
    • Production: 1962-1964
      Engine: 3.0L V12
      Power: 300 hp (220 kW)
      0-100 kph: 2.9 seconds
      Top Speed: 280 km/h
      Type: Grand Tourer
      Notable Feature: Only 36 units were produced, making it one of the rarest and most valuable Ferraris in the world.
    • The Ferrari Daytona SP3 is a limited-edition supercar that was unveiled in 2021. It is a modern interpretation of the classic Ferrari Daytona. The car is powered by a V12 engine and features a sleek, aerodynamic design.
    • Production: 2021-present
      Engine: 6.5L V12
      Power: 828 hp (618 kW)
      0-100 kph: 2.85 seconds
      Top Speed: 340 km/h
      Type: Supercar
      Notable Feature: Limited to 599 units, each car is custom-built to the owner’s specifications.
