McLaren

  • McLaren is a British luxury automaker known for its high-performance sports cars, founded in 1963 by Bruce McLaren. The company is headquartered in Woking, Surrey, and has earned a reputation for producing some of the fastest and most innovative cars in the world.
    Bruce McLaren (Founder)
  • The CEO of McLaren Automotive is Zak Brown, steering the brand toward cutting-edge performance, hybrid and electric innovation, and motorsport excellence. Under his leadership, McLaren continues to push the boundaries of lightweight engineering, aerodynamics, and high-performance supercars.
    Zak Brown (CEO)
  • McLaren has a rich history in motorsports, particularly in Formula 1, where its team, McLaren F1 Team, is one of the most successful in the sport’s history. The team has secured multiple Drivers’ and Constructors’ Championships, legendary victories, and developed some of the fastest and most technologically advanced race cars.
  • McLaren has been home to iconic drivers like Ayrton Senna, Alain Prost, Lewis Hamilton, and Mika Häkkinen. Beyond F1, the brand has also triumphed in endurance racing, winning prestigious events like the Le Mans 24 Hours with the McLaren F1 GTR.
  • McLaren's current road cars are renowned for their performance, innovation, and cutting-edge design. Iconic models like the McLaren P1, 720S, and Artura showcase the brand’s commitment to lightweight engineering, aerodynamics, and high-performance hybrid technology. With a focus on speed, precision, and driving dynamics, McLaren continues to push the boundaries of automotive excellence in the supercar and hypercar segments.
  • Here are some of McLaren's greatest creations :
    • The Mclaren P1 has hybrid powertrain for exceptional performance and efficiency. And light weight carbon fiber construction for agility and speed. Due to its limited production, the P1 is a highly sought-after collector's item.
    • Production: 2013-2015
      Engine: 3.8L twin-turbocharged V8 with electric motor
      Power: 903 hp (673 kW; 916 PS)
      0-100 kph: 2.8 seconds
      Top Speed: 349 km/h
      Type: Hybrid Hypercar
      Notable Feature: Active aerodynamics and Instant Power Assist System (IPAS)
    • The Mclaren 720S is a high-performance supercar known for its lightweight design, extreme aerodynamics, and blistering speed. Part of McLaren’s Super Series, it was introduced in 2017 as the successor to the 650S
    • Production: 2017-2023
      Engine: 4.0L twin-turbocharged V8
      Power: 710 hp (720 PS)
      0-100 kph: 2.9 seconds
      Top Speed: 341 km/h
      Type: Hybrid Hypercar
      Notable Feature: Active aerodynamics, Proactive Chassis Control II, dihedral doors
    • The Mclaren Senna is a track-focused hypercar named after legendary Formula 1 driver Ayrton Senna. It is designed to deliver the ultimate performance on the track while remaining road-legal.
    • Production: 2018-2019
      Engine: 4.0L twin-turbocharged V8
      Power: 789 hp (800 PS)
      0-100 kph: 2.8 seconds
      Top Speed: 335 km/h
      Type: Track-focused Hypercar
      Notable Feature: Active aerodynamics, carbon fiber construction, dihedral doors
    • The Mclaren Artura is a hybrid supercar that combines a 3.0L twin-turbo V6 with an electric motor for a total output of 671 hp. It is the first McLaren to use a new carbon fiber chassis and features an electric-only range of up to 19 miles.
    • Production: 2021-present
      Engine: 3.0L twin-turbocharged V6 with electric motor
      Power: 671 hp (680 PS)
      0-100 kph: 3.0 seconds
      Top Speed: 330 km/h
      Type: Hybrid Supercar
      Notable Feature: Carbon fiber chassis, electric-only mode, dihedral doors
