Aston Martin

  • Aston Martin is a British luxury sports car manufacturer known for its elegant design, high-performance vehicles, and rich heritage. Founded in 1913 by Lionel Martin and Robert Bamford, the company has been a symbol of British automotive excellence for over a century. The company is headquartered in Gaydon, Warwickshire, and is renowned for producing some of the most iconic cars in the world.
    Lionel Martin (Co-Founder)
  • As of 2025, the CEO of Aston Martin is Adrian Hallmark, leading the company into a new era of technological advancement, sustainability, and ultra-luxury performance vehicles.
    Adrian Hallmark (CEO)
  • Aston Martin has a deep-rooted history in motorsports, particularly in Formula 1, where the Aston Martin F1 Team is rapidly evolving into a top contender. With a strong legacy of engineering excellence, the team is committed to developing high-performance, aerodynamically advanced race cars that compete at the pinnacle of motorsport. Focused on innovation, precision, and strategic excellence, Aston Martin aims to achieve consistent podium finishes and establish itself as a powerhouse in the Formula 1 world.
  • Aston Martin has a rich history in motorsport, with legendary drivers contributing to its racing heritage. The brand has achieved significant success in endurance racing, including multiple class victories at the prestigious Le Mans 24 Hours. Aston Martin’s race cars, such as the DBR1 and the Vantage GTE, have cemented its reputation for performance and durability on some of the world's most challenging circuits.
  • Aston Martin's current road cars are celebrated for their blend of luxury, performance, and timeless design. Iconic models like the DB11, Vantage, and Valkyrie showcase the brand’s commitment to craftsmanship, aerodynamics, and cutting-edge engineering. With a focus on power, elegance, and driving experience, Aston Martin continues to set benchmarks in the world of grand tourers, supercars, and hypercars.
    • The Aston Martin DB11 is a grand tourer known for its striking design, refined luxury, and high-performance engine options.
    • Production: 2016-Present
      Engine: 4.0L twin-turbocharged V8 / 5.2L twin-turbocharged V12
      Power: 503 HP (V8) / 715 HP (V12)
      0-100 kph: 3.9 seconds (V8) / 3.7 seconds (V12)
      Top Speed: 334 km/h
      Type: Grand Tourer (GT)
      Notable Feature: Aeroblade aerodynamic system, luxurious interior
    • The Aston Martin Valkyrie is a hypercar developed in collaboration with Red Bull Racing, designed for extreme performance and aerodynamics.
    • Production: 2021-Present
      Engine: 6.5L naturally aspirated V12 with hybrid assistance
      Power: 1,160 HP
      0-100 kph: 2.5 seconds
      Top Speed: 402 km/h
      Type: Hypercar
      Notable Feature: Formula 1-inspired aerodynamics, lightweight construction
    • The Aston Martin Valhalla is a limited-production hypercar that combines the best of Aston Martin's design and engineering capabilities with the latest hybrid technology.
    • Production: 2022-Present
      Engine: 4.0L twin-turbocharged V8 with hybrid assistance
      Power: 937 HP
      0-100 kph: 2.5 seconds
      Top Speed: 330 km/h
      Type: Hypercar
      Notable Feature: Lightweight construction, advanced aerodynamics
